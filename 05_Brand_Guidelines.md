# Blueleaf Clinic — Brand Guidelines
## Mini Brand Standards Manual

## INTRODUCTION

These brand guidelines ensure consistency across all Blueleaf Clinic touchpoints. Whether you're creating a social media post, designing a brochure, or developing a website page, these standards keep our brand cohesive, professional, and recognizable.

**Purpose:** Maintain visual and verbal consistency that builds trust and recognition

**Who Should Use This:** Internal team members, designers, marketers, partners, vendors

**When to Use This:** Before creating any branded materials

## 2. LOGO USAGE RULES

### Primary Logo

**Full Lockup (Symbol + Wordmark)**

```
[Leaf Symbol]  BLUELEAF
               CLINIC
```

**When to Use:**
- Primary applications (website header, signage, business cards)
- When space allows for full lockup
- First brand introduction on any material

**Minimum Size:**
- Print: 1.5" wide
- Digital: 140px wide

---

### Logo Variations

#### **Horizontal Lockup**
Symbol left + Wordmark right (most common)

#### **Stacked Lockup**
Symbol top + Wordmark bottom (for square spaces, social avatars)

#### **Symbol Only**
Use when Blueleaf is already established in context (app icon, favicon, watermarks, secondary branding)

**Minimum Size:** 32px × 32px (digital) or 0.5" × 0.5" (print)

#### **Wordmark Only**
Use in text-heavy applications where symbol would be too small or distracting (email signatures, document headers)

---

### Logo Color Options

#### **Primary (Full Color)**
- Symbol: Blueleaf Blue (#2B7A9B)
- Wordmark: Deep Charcoal (#2C3E50)
- **Use:** White or light backgrounds

#### **Monochrome Blue**
- All elements: Blueleaf Blue (#2B7A9B)
- **Use:** Brand-colored backgrounds, special applications

#### **Monochrome White**
- All elements: White (#FFFFFF)
- **Use:** Dark backgrounds, photography overlays, colored backgrounds

#### **Monochrome Dark**
- All elements: Deep Charcoal (#2C3E50)
- **Use:** Black and white printing, formal documents

---

### Clear Space

**Rule:** Maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the "B" in BLUELEAF

```
    [B-height space on all sides]

          [LOGO]
```

**Why:** Ensures logo remains prominent and uncluttered

**No Exceptions:** Even in tight layouts, respect clear space

---

### Logo Placement

**Preferred Positions:**
- Top left (websites, documents, most materials)
- Top center (social media, covers, formal presentations)
- Bottom center (footers, closing pages)

**Avoid:**
- Buried in dense content
- Bottom right (reserved for CTAs and less important elements)
- Rotated or angled placements

---

### Logo Don'ts

#### ❌ **NEVER:**

1. **Change logo colors** outside of approved variations
   - No rainbow logos
   - No off-brand colors
   - No gradients within logo elements

2. **Distort or stretch** the logo
   - Always maintain proportions
   - Lock aspect ratio when resizing

3. **Rotate** the logo
   - Logo must always be horizontal or vertical (stacked)

4. **Add effects**
   - No drop shadows (exception: subtle shadow on busy photography, max 5% opacity)
   - No outlines or strokes
   - No glows or bevels

5. **Recreate or modify** logo elements
   - Don't redraw the leaf
   - Don't change fonts
   - Don't rearrange elements

6. **Place on busy backgrounds** without proper contrast
   - Use solid color background or subtle overlay

7. **Use low-resolution files**
   - Always use vector (SVG, AI, EPS) when possible
   - Minimum 300 DPI for print

---

### Logo File Formats

**Request from Brand Team:**
- **Vector:** AI, EPS, SVG (for print, large format, scalability)
- **Raster:** PNG (transparent background, digital use)
- **Raster:** JPG (with white background, email-safe)

**File Naming Convention:**
- `Blueleaf_Logo_Primary_FullColor.svg`
- `Blueleaf_Logo_Symbol_White.png`
- `Blueleaf_Logo_Wordmark_Blue.eps`

---

### Color Accessibility

**Contrast Requirements (WCAG AA Standard):**
- **Body text:** Minimum 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- **Large text (18px+ or 14px+ bold):** Minimum 3:1 contrast ratio

**Approved High-Contrast Pairings:**
- Deep Charcoal (#2C3E50) on White ✅
- Deep Charcoal on Warm Cream ✅
- White on Blueleaf Blue ✅
- White on Fresh Leaf Green ✅
- White on Sunrise Coral ✅

**Avoid:**
- Soft Gray on Warm Cream (too low contrast)
- Light Gray text (use only for truly secondary info)
- Colored text on colored backgrounds without testing contrast

**Tool:** Use contrast checker (WebAIM, Stark, etc.) before finalizing designs

## 4. TYPOGRAPHY STANDARDS

### Font Families

#### **Primary: Plus Jakarta Sans**
- **Source:** Google Fonts (free, open source)
- **Usage:** Headlines, buttons, navigation, emphasis
- **Weights:** Bold (700), SemiBold (600), Medium (500)
- **License:** SIL Open Font License (free for commercial use)

#### **Secondary: Inter**
- **Source:** Google Fonts (free, open source)
- **Usage:** Body text, captions, supporting content
- **Weights:** SemiBold (600), Medium (500), Regular (400)
- **License:** SIL Open Font License (free for commercial use)

---

### Typography Hierarchy

| Element | Font | Weight | Size (Desktop) | Size (Mobile) | Color |
|---------|------|--------|----------------|---------------|-------|
| **H1 Hero** | Plus Jakarta Sans | Bold (700) | 48-64px | 32-40px | Deep Charcoal or Blueleaf Blue |
| **H2 Section** | Plus Jakarta Sans | Bold (700) | 36-40px | 28-32px | Deep Charcoal or Blueleaf Blue |
| **H3 Subsection** | Plus Jakarta Sans | SemiBold (600) | 24-28px | 20-24px | Deep Charcoal |
| **H4 Supporting** | Plus Jakarta Sans | SemiBold (600) | 18-20px | 18-20px | Deep Charcoal or Soft Gray |
| **Body Regular** | Inter | Regular (400) | 16-18px | 16px | Deep Charcoal |
| **Body Emphasis** | Inter | Medium (500) | 16-18px | 16px | Deep Charcoal |
| **Small/Captions** | Inter | Regular (400) | 14px | 14px | Soft Gray |
| **Buttons/CTAs** | Plus Jakarta Sans | Medium (500) | 16-18px | 16-18px | White (on colored button) |
| **Navigation** | Plus Jakarta Sans | Medium (500) | 15-16px | 15-16px | Deep Charcoal or White |

---

### Typography Rules

#### **Line Height:**
- Headlines: 1.1 - 1.3
- Body text: 1.6 - 1.7
- Captions: 1.5

#### **Letter Spacing:**
- Headlines: -0.5px to 0px (tighter for large text)
- Body: 0px (default)
- Buttons/CTAs: +0.3px (slight tracking for readability)
- Small caps/labels: +1.5px to +2px

#### **Paragraph Spacing:**
- 16-20px between paragraphs
- 24px between headline and body text
- 40-60px between major sections

#### **Line Length:**
- Optimal: 50-75 characters per line
- Maximum: 90 characters
- Mobile: Full width with padding (16-24px sides)

#### **Alignment:**
- Body text: Left-aligned (never justified)
- Headlines: Left-aligned or centered (context-dependent)
- CTAs: Centered (within buttons)

#### **Emphasis:**
- Use weight changes (Medium, SemiBold) over italic
- Use color sparingly (Fresh Leaf Green for positive, Blueleaf Blue for important)
- Avoid all caps except for small labels/tags

---

### Web Font Loading

```css
/* Google Fonts Import */
@import url('https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Plus+Jakarta+Sans:wght@500;600;700&family=Inter:wght@400;500;600&display=swap');

/* Font Stack with Fallbacks */
font-family: 'Plus Jakarta Sans', 'Helvetica Neue', Arial, sans-serif;
font-family: 'Inter', 'Helvetica Neue', Arial, sans-serif;
```

**Fallback Fonts:** System sans-serifs ensure readability if custom fonts fail to load

---

### Print Typography Notes

- Use Plus Jakarta Sans and Inter (both free for print)
- Embed fonts in PDFs to ensure consistency
- Minimum 10pt for body text (12pt preferred)
- Increase leading (line-height) slightly for print (1.7-1.8 for body)

## 5. PHOTOGRAPHY & IMAGERY

### Photography Style

**Mood:**
- Warm, welcoming, authentic
- Bright and naturally lit (not overly clinical or sterile)
- Optimistic without being fake
- Professional but approachable

**Content:**
- Real people in genuine moments (not overly staged)
- Diverse representation (age, ethnicity, ability, family structures)
- Healthcare settings that feel modern and comfortable
- Patients and providers interacting warmly

**Technical:**
- High resolution (minimum 1920px wide for web, 300 DPI for print)
- Bright, natural lighting
- Soft focus/depth of field acceptable (keeps focus on subjects)
- Color grading: Slightly warm, cohesive with brand palette (avoid overly cool/blue tones)

---

### Photo Treatment

#### **Corners:**
- Rounded corners: 12-20px radius (depending on size)
- Never sharp 90° corners (too harsh)

#### **Borders:**
- Optional: 1px Light Gray border for definition on white backgrounds
- Or subtle shadow: `0px 2px 12px rgba(43, 122, 155, 0.08)`

#### **Overlays (when text is on photo):**
- Gradient overlay: White to transparent (for light text on photo)
- Or solid color overlay at 80-90% opacity (Blueleaf Blue, Deep Charcoal)
- Ensure text has minimum 4.5:1 contrast with photo background

---

### Image Categories

#### **Hero/Lifestyle Images:**
- Patients smiling, families together, healthcare interactions
- Use full-width or large format (impactful, emotional connection)

#### **Team Photos:**
- Doctors, nurses, staff in professional attire
- Friendly, approachable expressions
- Modern clinic background (not dated medical equipment)

#### **Environment/Clinic Photos:**
- Waiting rooms, exam rooms, reception areas
- Clean, bright, modern, comfortable
- No patients visible (privacy)

#### **Stock Photos (if necessary):**
- **Preferred sources:** Unsplash, Pexels (free), iStock, Adobe Stock (paid)
- **Avoid:** Overly staged stock photos with fake smiles
- **Tip:** Search for "authentic," "candid," "natural light," "diverse"

---

### Image Don'ts

#### ❌ **Avoid:**

1. **Overly clinical or sterile** environments (cold, institutional, intimidating)
2. **Dated photography** (old equipment, outdated fashion, poor quality)
3. **Fake or overly staged** moments (forced smiles, awkward poses)
4. **Low resolution or pixelated** images
5. **Dark, shadowy, or poorly lit** photos
6. **Photos that don't represent our community** (lack of diversity)
7. **Cheesy stock photo clichés** (thumbs up at camera, laughing at salad)

## 6. ICONOGRAPHY

### Icon Style

**Design:**
- Line icons (stroke-based, not filled/solid)
- Stroke weight: 2px
- Rounded line caps and corners (2-3px radius)
- Clean, modern, simple

**Personality:**
- Professional but friendly
- Not overly decorative or playful
- Functional and clear

---

### Icon Sizes

- **Large (Hero icons):** 96px (feature highlights, chapter openers)
- **Medium (Content icons):** 48px (section markers, callouts)
- **Standard (UI icons):** 32px (lists, features, benefits)
- **Small (Inline icons):** 16-24px (inline with text, metadata)

**Grid:** Design on 24px grid, scale proportionally

---

### Icon Colors

**Primary Use:**
- Blueleaf Blue (#2B7A9B) for medical/professional icons
- Fresh Leaf Green (#6DB89F) for wellness/preventive care icons
- Soft Gray (#6B7C8C) for neutral/UI icons
- Sunrise Coral (#E8927C) for action/CTA icons

**Special Contexts:**
- White icons on colored backgrounds
- Monochrome on photography
- Single color in dense UI (consistency)

---

### Icon Categories

**Medical:**
Stethoscope, heart with pulse, medical cross, prescription, thermometer, clipboard

**Services:**
Video call (telehealth), calendar (appointments), clock (hours), location pin, phone, document

**Wellness:**
Leaf, water drop, sun, apple/fruit, person active, sleep/moon, smile, balance

**UI/Navigation:**
Arrow, checkmark, X/close, menu, search, user, settings

---

### Icon Sources

- **Custom Design:** Preferred (ensures brand consistency)
- **Icon Libraries (if needed):** Feather Icons, Heroicons, Phosphor Icons (all have line-style options)
- **Customization:** Adjust stroke weight and radius to match brand (2px, rounded)

## 8. ACCESSIBILITY GUIDELINES

### Color Contrast

**Minimum Requirements (WCAG AA):**
- Normal text (under 18px): 4.5:1 contrast ratio
- Large text (18px+ or 14px+ bold): 3:1 contrast ratio
- UI elements and graphics: 3:1 contrast ratio

**Our High-Contrast Pairings:**
- Deep Charcoal on White: 15.6:1 ✅
- Deep Charcoal on Warm Cream: 14.8:1 ✅
- White on Blueleaf Blue: 4.9:1 ✅
- White on Fresh Leaf Green: 3.2:1 ✅ (large text only)
- White on Sunrise Coral: 3.8:1 ✅ (large text only)

**Test All Designs:** Use WebAIM Contrast Checker, Stark plugin, or built-in browser tools

---

### Typography Accessibility

**Minimum Sizes:**
- Body text: 16px (1rem) minimum
- Small text/captions: 14px minimum (use sparingly)

**Line Height:**
- Minimum 1.5 for body text
- Minimum 1.3 for headlines

**Line Length:**
- Maximum 90 characters per line
- Optimal: 50-75 characters

**Font Choices:**
- Sans-serif for screen (Inter, Plus Jakarta Sans)
- High x-height for readability
- Avoid thin or ultra-light weights

---

### Alternative Text for Images

**All images must include descriptive alt text:**

**Good Example:**
`alt="Dr. Sarah Patel smiling while talking with a patient in a bright exam room"`

**Bad Example:**
`alt="Doctor"` or `alt="IMG_1234"`

**Decorative Images:**
`alt=""` (empty alt tag tells screen readers to skip)

**Icons with Meaning:**
Include aria-label or alt text describing function
`aria-label="Schedule appointment"`

---

### Link Accessibility

**Clear Link Text:**
- ✅ "Book your appointment online"
- ❌ "Click here" or "Learn more" (no context)

**Keyboard Navigation:**
- All interactive elements must be keyboard accessible (tab, enter, space)
- Visible focus states (outline or background change)

**Link Colors:**
- Distinguish links from regular text (color + underline)
- Blueleaf Blue with underline for inline links

---

### Form Accessibility

- All form fields must have visible labels (not just placeholders)
- Error messages must be clear and specific
- Required fields clearly marked (asterisk + label text)
- Color not the only indicator of error (use icons + text)

---

### Video & Audio

- Captions for all video content
- Transcripts for audio content
- No auto-play with sound (respect user control)

## 9. DESIGN DO'S AND DON'TS

### ✅ DO:

#### **Visual Design:**
1. **Use ample white space** — Avoid cramming too much content into one design
2. **Maintain visual hierarchy** — Guide the eye with size, color, and weight
3. **Be consistent** — Use the same fonts, colors, and spacing throughout
4. **Prioritize readability** — Content must be easy to read and understand
5. **Use high-quality images** — Never use pixelated or low-res images
6. **Respect the grid** — Align elements for clean, professional layouts
7. **Keep it simple** — Less is more; remove unnecessary elements

#### **Brand Voice:**
1. **Be warm and professional** — Balance expertise with approachability
2. **Use conversational language** — Avoid medical jargon
3. **Show empathy** — Acknowledge patient concerns and feelings
4. **Be solution-focused** — Offer help, not just problems
5. **Use active voice** — "We help you" not "You will be helped"

#### **Accessibility:**
1. **Test color contrast** — Ensure all text is readable
2. **Write descriptive alt text** — Help screen reader users understand images
3. **Use proper heading hierarchy** — H1, H2, H3 in order
4. **Make links clear** — Link text should make sense out of context

---

### ❌ DON'T:

#### **Visual Design:**
1. **Don't distort the logo** — Always lock aspect ratio
2. **Don't use off-brand colors** — Stick to the approved palette
3. **Don't use more than 3-4 colors** in one design — Too many colors look chaotic
4. **Don't use low-contrast text** — If you squint to read it, it fails accessibility
5. **Don't overcrowd layouts** — Let content breathe with white space
6. **Don't use decorative fonts** — Stick to Plus Jakarta Sans and Inter
7. **Don't use busy backgrounds** behind text — Ensure readability first
8. **Don't use cheesy stock photos** — Authentic > overly staged

#### **Brand Voice:**
1. **Don't use medical jargon** without explanation — Make it understandable
2. **Don't be overly casual** — "Hey!" and slang are too informal
3. **Don't create fear or anxiety** — Be reassuring, not alarming
4. **Don't oversell** — Avoid exaggerated claims or hype
5. **Don't sound corporate** — Avoid robotic, buzzword-heavy language
6. **Don't dismiss patient concerns** — Always validate feelings

#### **Accessibility:**
1. **Don't use color alone** to convey meaning — Add text or icons
2. **Don't use tiny text** — 14px minimum, 16px preferred
3. **Don't auto-play videos with sound** — Respect user control
4. **Don't forget alt text** — Every meaningful image needs description

## BRAND GUIDELINES SUMMARY

These guidelines are designed to keep Blueleaf Clinic's brand consistent, professional, and recognizable across all touchpoints. When in doubt:

**Ask yourself:**
1. Does this feel warm and professional?
2. Is it accessible and easy to understand?
3. Does it align with our color, typography, and imagery standards?
4. Would this build trust with our patients?

If yes to all four—you're on brand. If unsure, refer back to these guidelines or reach out to the brand team.
