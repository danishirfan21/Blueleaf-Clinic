## 1. BRAND NAME MEANING & STORY

### The Name: Blueleaf

**Blue** represents:
- Trust, reliability, and professionalism in healthcare
- Calm, healing, and peace of mind
- Clarity and communication
- Medical expertise (traditional healthcare blue)

**Leaf** represents:
- Growth, vitality, and natural wellness

- The nurturing of families through every life stage

### Brand Story

Blueleaf Clinic was founded on the belief that modern healthcare should feel both trustworthy and approachable. We saw families struggling to find a medical home that balanced clinical excellence with genuine human connection.

Our name reflects this dual promise: the professionalism and expertise patients expect (Blue), combined with the natural, nurturing care they deserve (Leaf). We're not just treating symptoms‚Äîwe're helping families grow healthier, together.

## 6. LOGO CONCEPT

### Primary Logo: Symbol + Wordmark

#### SYMBOL: The Blueleaf Mark
**Visual Description:**
- A stylized leaf formed by two overlapping shapes creating a subtle heart silhouette
- The left side is a curved, organic leaf shape
- The right side mirrors it with a slight medical cross integration (subtle, not obvious)
- The overlap creates negative space in the center suggesting connection and care
- Smooth, rounded edges‚Äîmodern but not clinical
- Can stand alone as an icon or paired with wordmark

**Style Notes:**
- Clean, minimalist vector design
- Balanced and symmetrical for trust
- Organic curves for approachability
- Sophisticated enough for medical credibility
- Scalable from app icon to building signage

#### WORDMARK: Blueleaf Clinic
**Typography:**
- "BLUELEAF" in a custom-weighted modern sans-serif
- Slightly rounded letterforms (friendly but professional)
- Generous letter spacing for openness and clarity
- "CLINIC" as a subtle subtext or secondary lockup

**Font Character:**
- Geometric foundation with humanist touches
- Medium weight for strength without heaviness
- Clean, contemporary, accessible
- Professional but not corporate

### Logo Variations

**Primary Lockup:** Symbol left + Wordmark right (horizontal)

- **Full Color:** Blue symbol, full color wordmark

## 8. TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

### Headline Font: **PLUS JAKARTA SANS**

**Reason:** Modern, geometric sans-serif with friendly rounded curves. Highly readable, professional yet approachable. Extensive weight range for hierarchy.

**Weights & Usage:**
- **Bold (700):** Main headlines, hero text, section titles
- **SemiBold (600):** Subheadings, card titles, emphasis
- **Medium (500):** Navigation, buttons, labels

**Style Notes:**
- Slightly rounded terminals for warmth
- Excellent screen rendering
- Professional without being corporate
- Works beautifully at large sizes

### Body Font: **INTER**

**Reason:** Exceptional readability at all sizes, designed for screen display. Neutral, clear, highly accessible. Industry-standard for modern digital products.

**Weights & Usage:**
- **Regular (400):** Body text, paragraphs, long-form content
- **Medium (500):** Emphasized body text, list items, important information
- **SemiBold (600):** Captions, metadata, small headings

**Style Notes:**
- Optimized for digital reading
- Excellent legibility at small sizes
- Neutral and functional
- Pairs beautifully with Plus Jakarta Sans

### Typography Hierarchy

#### **H1 - Hero Headlines**
- Font: Plus Jakarta Sans Bold (700)
- Size: 48-64px (desktop) / 32-40px (mobile)
- Line Height: 1.1
- Letter Spacing: -0.5px
- Color: Deep Charcoal or Blueleaf Blue
- Usage: Landing pages, hero sections, main page titles

#### **H2 - Section Headers**
- Font: Plus Jakarta Sans Bold (700)
- Size: 36-40px (desktop) / 28-32px (mobile)
- Line Height: 1.2
- Letter Spacing: -0.3px
- Color: Deep Charcoal or Blueleaf Blue
- Usage: Section breaks, major content divisions

#### **H3 - Subsection Titles**
- Font: Plus Jakarta Sans SemiBold (600)
- Size: 24-28px (desktop) / 20-24px (mobile)
- Line Height: 1.3
- Letter Spacing: 0px
- Color: Deep Charcoal
- Usage: Card headers, content blocks, article titles

#### **H4 - Supporting Headlines**
- Font: Plus Jakarta Sans SemiBold (600)
- Size: 18-20px
- Line Height: 1.4
- Letter Spacing: 0px
- Color: Deep Charcoal or Soft Gray
- Usage: Small cards, list headers, secondary content

#### **Body - Regular Text**
- Font: Inter Regular (400)
- Size: 16-18px (desktop) / 16px (mobile)
- Line Height: 1.6
- Letter Spacing: 0px
- Color: Deep Charcoal
- Usage: All body copy, paragraphs, descriptions

#### **Body - Emphasized**
- Font: Inter Medium (500)
- Size: 16-18px
- Line Height: 1.6
- Color: Deep Charcoal
- Usage: Important body text, callouts within paragraphs

#### **Small Text / Captions**
- Font: Inter Regular (400)
- Size: 14px
- Line Height: 1.5
- Color: Soft Gray
- Usage: Captions, metadata, timestamps, secondary information

#### **Buttons & CTAs**
- Font: Plus Jakarta Sans Medium (500)
- Size: 16-18px
- Line Height: 1.2
- Letter Spacing: 0.3px
- Text Transform: None (sentence case)
- Usage: All interactive elements

#### **Navigation**
- Font: Plus Jakarta Sans Medium (500)
- Size: 15-16px
- Line Height: 1.4
- Letter Spacing: 0.2px
- Usage: Main navigation, menu items

### Typographic Rules

**Spacing:**
- Minimum 24px between headline and body text
- 16px between paragraphs
- 40-60px between major sections

**Line Length:**
- Body text: 50-75 characters per line (optimal readability)
- Narrow for mobile (full screen width with padding)

**Emphasis:**
- Use weight changes (medium/semibold) over italic
- Use color sparingly for emphasis (Fresh Leaf Green for positive, Blueleaf Blue for important)
- Avoid all caps except for small labels or tags

**Accessibility:**
- Minimum 16px font size for body text
- Minimum 1.5 line-height for readability
- All text must meet WCAG AA contrast ratios
- Never use color as the only means of conveying information

## 10. GRAPHIC STYLES, SHAPES & PATTERNS

### Shape Language

**Primary Shapes:**
- **Rounded rectangles** (12-16px border radius) for cards, buttons, containers
- **Soft circles** for icons, avatars, testimonial images
- **Organic curves** inspired by the leaf motif (flowing, natural)

**Avoid:**
- Sharp corners (too harsh, clinical)
- Perfect squares (too rigid)
- Excessive geometric complexity

### Visual Elements

#### **1. Leaf Patterns**
**Usage:** Subtle background patterns, section dividers, texture
**Style:** Deconstructed leaf shapes in light opacity (5-10%)
**Colors:** Fresh Leaf Green or Soft Teal at low opacity
**Application:** Website backgrounds, brochure accents, presentation slides

#### **2. Flowing Lines**
**Usage:** Connective elements, transitions between sections
**Style:** Smooth, curved lines suggesting growth and flow
**Colors:** Gradient from Blueleaf Blue to Fresh Leaf Green
**Application:** Hero sections, page dividers, infographics

#### **3. Dotted Grids (Subtle)**
**Usage:** Background texture for depth
**Style:** Small dots in grid pattern, very low opacity (3-5%)
**Colors:** Blueleaf Blue or Light Gray
**Application:** Section backgrounds, cards, landing page areas

#### **4. Photography Treatment**
**Style:** Soft rounded corners (16-20px radius), subtle shadow or border
**Overlay:** Optional light gradient overlay (white to transparent) for text legibility
**Content:** Real patients/families (with model releases), diverse representation
**Mood:** Bright, natural lighting; warm and welcoming; authentic moments

#### **5. Gradients (Use Sparingly)**
**Primary Gradient:**
- From Blueleaf Blue (#2B7A9B) to Soft Teal (#4A9BAA)
- Direction: 135¬∞ diagonal
- Usage: Hero backgrounds, CTA buttons, special sections

**Secondary Gradient:**
- From Fresh Leaf Green (#6DB89F) to Sunshine Yellow (#F4CF6D)
- Usage: Wellness content, health tips, positive messaging

**Rules:**
- Keep gradients subtle (not overpowering)
- Ensure text legibility over gradients
- Use for impact, not decoration

### Composition Principles

**Balance:**
- Asymmetric layouts with visual weight distribution
- Generous white (or warm cream) space
- 60-40 or 70-30 content splits

**Rhythm:**
- Consistent spacing using 8px grid system
- Predictable patterns for user comfort
- Break patterns intentionally for emphasis

**Hierarchy:**
- Size + color + weight to establish importance
- Lead with visuals, support with text
- Clear primary, secondary, tertiary levels
